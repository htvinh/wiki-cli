## Body
Non-Conformance Report Procedure
Purpose
This procedure defines the process for identifying, documenting, and resolving
non-conformances in products, processes, or the quality management system.
Scope
This procedure applies to all non-conformances discovered during production,
inspection, auditing, or customer feedback at Acme Corporation.
Definitions
NCR:Non-ConformanceReport—aformaldocumentthatrecordsadeviation
from specified requirements.
Disposition: The action taken on a non-conforming product (use-as-is, rework,
repair, scrap).
Root Cause: The underlying cause of a non-conformance.
Procedure
Step 1: Detection and Containment
When a non-conformance is detected (by operator, inspector, auditor, or cus-
tomer):
1. Immediatelystopproductionifthenon-conformanceaffectsproductsafety
or critical parameters.
2. Quarantine affected products with a red “QUARANTINED” tag.
3. Notify the QA Supervisor within 30 minutes.
4. Do not move or process quarantined material until QA disposition.
Step 2: Initiate NCR
The QA Supervisor or QA Engineer initiates an NCR in the QMS system:
1. Open the NCR template in the QMS portal.
2. Record the following information:
• Date and time of detection
• Product name and batch/lot number
• Quantity affected
• Description of the non-conformance
• Detected by (name and role)
• Production line or area
3. Assign a unique NCR number (format: NCR-YYYY-XXXX).
4. Attach supporting evidence (photos, test results, inspection records).
